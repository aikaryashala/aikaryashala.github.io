AI Impact Summit — Perspectives

Key Insights from the Discussion

NVIDIA's Incubation program is good. For startups, hardware is provided.

Every company is looking at how to extract data from systems.

Enterprises are looking for air-gapped solutions.

Internet vs Intranet — understanding the distinction matters for enterprise AI deployment.

Wrapper-only startups might become a feature of Claude Code or other major companies, so try to build non-wrapper solutions around AI APIs.

Software creation has been democratized by AI.

Connections and ideas will make people successful.

Ease of using the solution can be a differentiator.

Finding the gap: In your circle, why are the business people not using AI? If you enquire, you might find the gap and fill it — the business person will use it because you are more trustworthy than companies and corporations.

Tools & Concepts Mentioned

OpenClaw, NanoClaw — discussed as relevant tools/frameworks.

SEO & Discovery: Once you launch your website, Google updates its index in ~7 days. To get faster visibility, an example is getting your work listed at the top of Hacker News.

Domaining — the practice of buying and selling domain names was discussed.

OpenWRT, DD-WRT — open-source router firmware projects discussed in the context of networking and custom hardware.

1.58-bit Ternary Quantization — discussed as a technique for extreme model compression.

Running a Node.js server on mobile — some work and experiments around this were discussed.
